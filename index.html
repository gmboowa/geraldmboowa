About Me

I am a scientist dedicated to applying genome sequencing technologies to enhance our understanding of health and diseases globally. My work focuses on addressing key challenges such as pathogen characterization, outbreak investigation, and antimicrobial resistance (AMR) surveillance, leveraging advancements in data science, genomics, and bioinformatics to generate insights that inform and transform health outcomes worldwide.

Funded Research Projects

As an investigator, I have contributed to various research projects, including:

  • Understanding transmission dynamics and acquisition of antimicrobial resistance in East Africa at referral hospitals and community settings (details).
  • Piloting the use of rapid RT-qPCR assays for surveillance of SARS-CoV-2 variants of concern in Africa (details).
  • Genomic surveillance of Vibrio cholerae, Escherichia coli, and Klebsiella pneumoniae in Africa (details).
  • The European and Developing Countries Clinical Trials Partnership (EDCTP) project focused on pathogen detection in HIV-infected children with non-malarial febrile illnesses using metagenomic sequencing (details).

Recent Research Focus: Machine Learning in AMR Prediction

Our team is currently applying machine learning techniques to predict antimicrobial resistance (AMR) in bacterial pathogens. Recent studies include:

  • Escherichia coli: Using genomic data to predict resistance phenotypes and identify novel resistance determinants (full study).
  • Mycobacterium tuberculosis: Leveraging machine learning to enhance resistance prediction and support TB control efforts (full study).

Professional Contributions

Over the past 15 years, I have effectively combined roles in research and teaching. In collaboration with colleagues at Makerere University, I played a key role in establishing Fogarty/NIH-funded bioinformatics graduate training programs aimed at building genomics and research capacity across Africa. Additionally, I have organized numerous training programs to further support these efforts such as;

  • UC Berkeley’s Center for Emerging and Neglected Diseases
  • H3ABioNet
  • Wellcome Advanced Courses
  • Africa CDC

Bioinformatics Tools

My team developed two bioinformatics tools:

  • rMAP: A rapid microbial analysis and profiling pipeline (tool features, publication).
  • HIVDRIVES: For HIV drug resistance profiling (details).

My Interests

  • HIV Drug Resistance Analysis

    Leveraging genomic technologies and bioinformatics tools to identify genetic markers of resistance, understand resistance mechanisms, and generate data-driven insights to inform treatment strategies, optimize therapeutic outcomes, and guide public health interventions against HIV drug resistance.

  • AMR Genomic Surveillance

    Using genomic technologies and bioinformatics tools for real-time detection, monitoring, and analysis of antimicrobial resistance (AMR) determinants to inform evidence-based treatment guidelines, public health policies, and global efforts against resistant pathogens.

  • Pathogen Genomics

    Applying genomic technologies and bioinformatics to understand pathogen diversity, evolution, and transmission dynamics, enabling rapid identification, characterization, and control of infectious diseases to strengthen global health security.

  • Bioinformatics Capacity Building

    Empowering individuals, institutions, and regions with the skills, infrastructure, and collaborative networks necessary to advance research, address public health challenges, and implement impactful bioinformatics solutions focusing on sustainability, equity, and local relevance.
