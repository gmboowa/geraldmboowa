About Me

I am a scientist passionate about applying genome sequencing technologies to tackle challenges in health and disease, including pathogen characterization, outbreak investigation, and antimicrobial resistance (AMR) surveillance. My research aims to contribute to data science, genomics, and bioinformatics to improve health outcomes globally.

Projects

  • HIV Drug Resistance Analysis

    Leverage genomic technologies and bioinformatics tools for identifying genetic markers of resistance, understanding resistance mechanisms, and generating data-driven insights to inform effective treatment strategies, optimize therapeutic outcomes, and guide public health interventions in combating HIV drug resistance.

  • AMR Genomic Surveillance

    Effectively leverage genomic technologies and bioinformatics tools for real-time detection, monitoring, and analysis of AMR determinants, enabling evidence-based decision-making to inform treatment guidelines, public health policies, and global efforts to combat the spread of resistant pathogens.

  • Pathogen Genomics

    Harness genomic technologies and bioinformatics tools for understanding pathogen diversity, evolution, and transmission dynamics, enabling rapid identification, characterization, and control of infectious diseases to improve public health outcomes and strengthen global health security.

  • Bioinformatics Capacity Building

    Empower individuals, institutions, and regions with the skills, infrastructure, and collaborative networks necessary to effectively utilize bioinformatics tools and approaches for advancing scientific research, addressing public health challenges, and translating data-driven insights into impactful solutions, with a focus on sustainability, equity, and local relevance.
